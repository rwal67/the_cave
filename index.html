The streets are so clean they almost reflect any happenings here on the surface, pedestrians find below them their own personal doppelganger, walking in tandem with them.


The sky is blotted out by skyscrapers, the stars hide from man, trees and plants know their place, staying contently in their cement homes.


It is just shy of 8 in the morning. Neat and orderly do we all make our commutes; every warm body looks steadfastly ahead. I, myself, am on my way to my shift at the intake processing plant. A respectable position, with gracious benefits, I fought tooth and nail to land this.

First undergraduate, then graduate studies, and through scores of internships and apprenticeships- I learned everything there is to know about the filing of lines of people, who stands in line, what brought them to the line, the optimal orientation of a line, and the very philosophy a line-organizer should carry with them to perform to the best of their abilities.
