It is a gentle 68 degrees fahrenheit outside; the vents so lovingly laid out across every other building facade and sidewalk tend to this. In front of me, the workers steadily travel in a perfectly winding line, the path to follow determined by the one in front of each, a signal of mutual dependence and trust- a textbook, organic occurrence of Vostrikov's second theory of queue.

This I know well, having even written my thesis, "towards a contemporary understanding of Vostrikov's line", on the subject.

  • The Principle of Temporal Equality "Each individual in the queue is bound by an equal share of the collective waiting, yet none can experience time identically. The act of standing in line imposes a communal burden, but the perception of that burden is infinitely subjective."
  • The Law of Positional Integrity "Ones place in the queue is both a reflection of the selss immediate moral standing and an assertion of future potential. To abandon or violate this place is to disrupt the inherent metaphysical balance of progression."
  • The Paradox of Terminal Proximity "As one nears the terminal point of the queue, the anticipated conclusion recedes into mere abstraction. The queue, in its essence, is not a finite construct, but rather a continuous loop. One in which the terminal point merely reconfigures into another beginning."

I check my watch, its just 5 minutes shy of 8 in the morning. Then checking my phone, it says the same thing. My commute is a short one; it is a short commute for everybody in this line. If the commute were any longer, they would have left hours ago. The terminal looms overhead, a mass of brutalist, titanic, intersecting concrete- like a mass of industrious tentacles would be considered uninviting to less refined individuals. But to me, this is like waking up and seeing your loved spouse's face every morning.


My marriage to efficiency is a happy one.
